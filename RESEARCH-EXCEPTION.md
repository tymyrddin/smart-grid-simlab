# Research Exception

*"The truth may be out there, but lies are inside your head."* -- Terry Pratchett, Hogfather

Notwithstanding the [Polyform Noncommercial Licence](LICENCE), the author explicitly permits use of this software for legitimate academic and educational research, free of charge.

## Who this is for

The Smart Grid Simlab exists to make people think: about how critical infrastructure fails, how operators and institutions respond under pressure, how small technical compromises become large societal disruptions, and how people in positions of responsibility behave when the grid is going dark and the phones are ringing.

If your work involves any of the following, you are welcome here without a commercial licence:

- Academic research into ICS security, grid resilience, or critical infrastructure protection
- Coursework, dissertations, or theses at any level of education
- Classroom use by educators incorporating the simulator into curricula
- Non-commercial conference presentations or demonstrations
- Cybersecurity research using simulation as a methodological tool
- Policy research into energy security or national infrastructure risk
- Tabletop exercises run by non-commercial community, civic, or public sector groups
- Serious games scholarship or educational game design research

## What we ask in return

We ask nothing legally binding. We ask, in the spirit of good-faith collaboration, that you:

- Attribute the simulator in any publication or presentation
- Share findings that might improve the software, if you are so inclined
- Do not present the simulation as more realistic or authoritative than it is

## What this exception does not cover

This exception does not cover:

- Paid courses, workshops, or training programmes, even in academic institutions
- Consulting or advisory work delivered for a fee
- Integration into commercial products or platforms

If your research transitions into paid delivery, a [commercial licence](COMMERCIAL-LICENCE.md) is required.

## In the grey areas

If your use case seems to fall between "obviously fine" and "obviously commercial", [reach out](https://tymyrddin.dev/contact/), and we will work something out sensibly, without excessive paperwork.
